EVENTS

Health Expo and Wellness Fair June,24,2024

The university hosts an annual Health Expo and Wellness Fair, bringing together students, faculty, staff, and the local community to promote health and well-being. The event features interactive exhibits, health screenings, fitness demonstrations, nutrition workshops, and talks by healthcare professionals. Students from various health sciences disciplines showcase their research projects and community outreach initiatives, highlighting the university's commitment to promoting healthy lifestyles and preventive care.

Simulation-Based Training Workshops: Upcoming

The university organizes simulation-based training workshops for students enrolled in health sciences programs such as nursing, medicine, pharmacy, and allied health professions. These workshops provide hands-on experience using high-fidelity simulation equipment and virtual reality technology to simulate real-life clinical scenarios. Students practice critical skills, decision-making, and teamwork in a safe and controlled environment, preparing them for clinical rotations and future healthcare careers.

Interdisciplinary Research Symposium: Upcoming

The university hosts an interdisciplinary research symposium focused on health sciences, where faculty and students present their latest research findings and collaborate on innovative projects. The symposium covers a wide range of topics including biomedical research, public health interventions, healthcare policy, and clinical innovations. It fosters collaboration between different departments and research centers within the university, as well as with external partners in the healthcare industry and academia, driving advancements in healthcare knowledge and practice.

History

The University of Health Sciences was founded in 2014 with a vision to address the growing need for skilled healthcare professionals. Originally established as a small institution offering medical education, it has since expanded its programs to include nursing, public health, allied health sciences, and interdisciplinary research. Over the years, the university has earned a reputation for excellence in education, research, and community service, attracting students and faculty from diverse backgrounds and contributing to advancements in healthcare regionally and globally. Today, the University of Health Sciences continues to uphold its commitment to training compassionate and competent healthcare professionals while remaining at the forefront of healthcare innovation and advocacy.Our institution was founded in 2014, and since then, we have been dedicated to academic excellence, research, and community engagement.
The University of Health Sciences stands as a beacon of knowledge and innovation in the realm of healthcare education. With a commitment to excellence, it cultivates the next generation of healthcare professionals, equipping them with the skills, knowledge, and compassion needed to make a meaningful impact in the lives of individuals and communities. Through cutting-edge research, interdisciplinary collaboration, and a dedication to serving humanity, the university is at the forefront of advancing healthcare practices and improving global well-being

Admissions Requirement

CANDIDATES WITH WASSCE/SSSCE

Minimum/General Entry Requirements

An applicant for admission to a degree programme in the University must have CREDIT passes (A1 - C6 in WASSCE and A – D in SSSCE) in the THREE WASSCE/SSSCE Core Subjects (English Language, Mathematics, and Integrated Science), and CREDIT passes in THREE WASSCE/SSSCE Elective Subjects or its equivalent relevant to the chosen programme with aggregate 24 or better. It is worth noting that the THREE Core Subjects are prerequisite for all Programmes of study at our University

GCE/IGCSE (CAMBRIDGE) ‘A’ & ‘O’LEVELS/MATURE APPLICANTS

Holders of GCE/IGCSE must have FIVE credits at the ‘O’ Level including English Language and Mathematics (or its approved equivalent), plus passes in at least THREE (3) subjects at the Advanced Level/Three Credits in the case of mature applicants. In addition, the applicant must fulfil the appropriate Faculty or Departmental requirements.

GENERAL BUSINESS CERTIFICATE EXAMINATION (GBCE)/ ADVANCED BUSINESS CERTIFICATE EXAMINATION (ABCE)

ABCE and GBCE are both needed to qualify an applicant for a programme. Holders of GBCE must have passes in THREE Compulsory Subjects including English Language and passes in THREE Elective Subjects including Business Mathematics. Holders ABCE must have passes in THREE Compulsory Subjects plus THREE (3) Optional Subjects relevant to the Programme of study.

INTERNATIONAL BACCALAUREATE (IB)

Holders of International Baccalaureate (IB) should have a minimum of grade 4 at the Higher Level (HL) in Three (3) Subjects relevant to the Programme of choice and minimum 7 of grade 5 in Chemistry for Health and Allied Sciences. For Engineering, at least grade 5 at the Higher level (HL) in Mathematics.
In addition, one must have a minimum of grade 4 in English Language and Mathematics at the Standard Level (SL) and at least grade C in a Science subject at the IGCSE

AMERICAN HIGH SCHOOL GRADE 12

An applicant for admission to a degree programme in the University of Health Science must have a Final Grade Point of at least 3.0. For admission to the University School of Medicine and Dentistry, an applicant must have a Final Grade Point of at least 3.25. Admissions will be to the Year 1
